Down The Ladder

The birds successfully climb down the ladder into the sewers.
Water drips from the ceiling, echoing through the dim, mossy corridor. Pipes groan quietly as the group steps onto the damp floor, their feathers slightly ruffled by the descent.
Chuck peers around the corner. “Smells like mystery and bad plumbing,” he whispers.
Matilda: Keep your eyes open. Creeper Jack won’t make it easy to reclaim the citrine gemstone.
As they proceed cautiously, Bomb accidentally steps on a soft patch of floor. It hisses, then clicks.
Red raises his wing. “Wait… did anyone else hear that?”
A loud rumble answers him.
From the darkness, a wall panel rotates open to reveal a surveillance camera blinking red.
“I think we’re being watched,” Blue gulps.
Suddenly, two paths light up: one leads to a locked vault door with a keypad, and the other to a tunnel marked with old symbols.
What should the birds do next?
